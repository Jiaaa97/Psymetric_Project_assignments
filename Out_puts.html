Introduction

The study focuses on investigating changes in life satisfaction among young adults across two time points, 1987 and 1991. Life satisfaction was measured with three indicators at each time point: “In most respects my life is ideal,” “My living conditions are excellent,” and “Overall I am satisfied with my life.”. Additionally, we performed a longitudinal confirmatory factor analysis(CFA) to assess the relationship between the life satisfaction factors for both years and determine whether life satisfaction of young adults changed overtime.

The empirical question is whether life satisfaction has improved or deteriorated during this period. The results of these analyses will allow us to draw conclusions about general trends in life satisfaction among the survey participants.

Method

Dataset

  • Data description: The data comes from a survey of young adults aged 17-27 in 1987 and reassessed in 1991. There are 1775 responses regarding life satisfaction, they were convert into a likert scale with values ranging from 1 to 7. There are few missing values.

Variables: Life satisfaction indicators for both years include the following:

Statistical Analysis

Missingness

The data included missing values for indicators which followed a completely random missing (MCAR) mechanism. To address this problem, these missing values were removed by using a listwise approach. This led to a final sample of 1,245 participants for analysis. All indicator Likert scale responses are assumed to be numerical. After data preparation, descriptive statistics (mean, standard deviation, covariance) were computed for all indicators.

Identification

It is crucial to examine whether the model is identifiable or not, because the identifiability refers to whether the data provide enough information to uniquely estimate the model parameters in the background of SEM. A model is identifiable if it has positive degrees of freedom, meaning that there are more known data points (variances and covariances) than there are parameters to be estimated.

Based on the formula in the slide, \[ df = 1/2(p(p+1)) - q\]. We get the total estimated parameters are: \[ q = 6 \text{(factor loadings)} + 6 \text{(observed variances)} + 1 \text{(latent factor covariance)} + 3 \text{(error covariances)} = 16 \] And the degrees of freedom are: \[ df = 1/2(6(6+1) - 15 = 21 - 16 = 5 \]. The degrees of freedom are indeed positive, indicating the model is over-identified with more known than unknown information. And this allows us to proceed with testing for measurement invariance.

Longitudinal Factor Analysis

  • Configural Invariance: To observe whether the same factor structure is consistent at different time points between 1987 and 1991 without restricting any parameters.

  • Metric Invariance: To test the equality of factor loadings across time points, testing whether the relationships between the observed items and the latent construct Life Satisfaction are consistent between 1987 and 1991 by constraining the factor loadings to be equal across time.

  • Scalar Invariance: To test the equality of item intercepts across time points, ensuring that the means of the indicators are comparable over time.

Discussion

Since the metric model does not include the constraints of intercepts of the observed variables, this means it allows for differences in intercepts across time. I.e. we cannot compare the means of the life satisfaction between 1987 and 1991 in the metric model, because the baseline levels of life satisfaction indicators in 1987 versus in 1991 are not constrained and they could vary. And if we want to test whether life satisfaction has improved or deteriorated from 1987 to 1991, we would need to establish scalar invariance, where both factor loadings and intercepts are constrained to be equal across time.

In conclusion, although life satisfaction was measured consistently in terms of its relationships with observed variables across 1987 and 1991, due to the lack of scalar invariance, we could not definitively conclude whether ‘Life Satisfaction’ improves or deteriorates from 1987 to 1991.
